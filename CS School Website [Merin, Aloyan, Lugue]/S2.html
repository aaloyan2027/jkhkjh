SECTION 2: SIMULATION OF ABSORPTION


Simulation of Absorption


  In this experiment, we tried to simulate how the small intestines absorb nutrients by using tissue paper. We had four graduated cylinders with equal amounts of water and then prepared four tissue paper but with increasing amounts of folds. After dipping the tissues in each graduated cylinder, we found that the tissue with the most folds absorbed the most water. This explains why our small intestines have folds on the surface since the folds increase the surface area and thus result in more nutrients being absorbed.
