SECTION 1: CRUSHING STAGE


Crushing Stage Experiment


  In the experiment, we used food such as bananas and crackers and placed them in a zip-locked bag to simulate digestion happening in the stomach. The water we added next imitates the salivary amylase produced by the salivary glands in the oral cavity to break down starches before the food enters the stomach. Crushing the mixture represents the mechanical digestion happening in the stomach in the initial stages of digestion. The biomolecules present in the food are glucose, fiber, fat, and protein. We then added calamansi juice to simulate the gastric juice secreted by the stomach which kills bacteria and denatures proteins because of its acidity.
